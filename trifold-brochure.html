Understanding the Condition
What Is SETD5
Syndrome?

SETD5 Syndrome is a rare genetic neurodevelopmental condition caused by a variant in the SETD5 gene on chromosome 3p25.3. One working copy of the gene instead of two disrupts development in ways that can affect cognition, behavior, communication, and physical features.

Genetic Location

SETD5 gene · Chromosome 3p25.3 · Autosomal dominant · Histone methyltransferase critical to brain development

How It Occurs Most cases arise as a de novo (spontaneous) mutation — not inherited from either parent. ~10–20% are inherited.
Diagnosis Chromosomal microarray (CMA) or whole-exome/genome sequencing (WES/WGS).
First Described 2014 · Research is growing as genetic testing expands globally.
Also known as
MRD23 ADID 23 SETD5-Related Disorder 3p25.3 Microdeletion

"Every person with SETD5 Syndrome is unique. Our mission is to fully understand this condition and support those living with it."

Reported Features

This is not an exhaustive list. Features are drawn from published case series; individual presentation varies.

Learning & Cognition

Intellectual disability · Developmental delay · Working memory difficulties

Speech & Language

Speech delays · Language delays · Expressive language often more affected than receptive

Behavior & Regulation

ADHD features · Anxiety · Sensory sensitivities · Autism spectrum features

Neurological & Motor

Hypotonia · Motor delays · Sleep disturbances · Epilepsy (reported in some)

Social Communication

Social communication differences · Reduced safety awareness (reported in some)

Characteristics listed are drawn from published case series (2014–2024). Individual presentation varies significantly. This information is educational only.
