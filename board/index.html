Foundation Setup Roadmap

Steps to complete now that we have our Virginia Certificate of Incorporation.

Virginia Certificate of Incorporation — effective March 4, 2026
Needs verification against Virginia law before relying on. This roadmap reflects general nonprofit formation practice and should be checked against the Virginia Nonstock Corporation Act (Title 13.1), the Virginia State Corporation Commission, and current IRS guidance before treating any step as final. Note: Virginia charitable solicitation registration requirements are not covered here and may apply. Confirm your specific obligations with a Virginia nonprofit attorney.
Completed
Do this now
Next up
After 501(c)(3)
Phase 1 — Legal & Financial Foundation
Virginia Certificate of Incorporation
Done ✓
The foundation is a legally recognized nonprofit corporation in the Commonwealth of Virginia. Effective March 4, 2026.
Unlocks: EIN application Foundation bank account
2
Apply for EIN (Employer Identification Number)
Do This Now
Your federal tax ID number. Takes about 10 minutes online and you receive it immediately. This is not the same as 501(c)(3) status — you do not need to wait for a letter from the IRS first.
Time: ~10 minutes
Need: Certificate of Incorporation, responsible party's SSN
Where: irs.gov → "Apply for EIN Online" → Other Nonprofit Organization
Unlocks: Nonprofit bank account Donation platforms 501(c)(3) application
3
Open a Nonprofit Bank Account
After EIN
A dedicated bank account in the foundation's name. Required before any donation platform can deposit funds. Look for accounts with no monthly fees — online banks like Relay are popular with small nonprofits.
Options: Relay (online, free), local credit union, Chase Nonprofit
Need: EIN, Certificate of Incorporation, bylaws (some banks), board resolution
Unlocks: Receiving donations Paying foundation expenses
Phase 2 — Governing Documents & Donations
4
Draft & Adopt Bylaws
Can Start Now
Your bylaws govern how the foundation operates — board structure, voting, meetings, officer roles, and dissolution. Can be worked on in parallel with the EIN and bank account steps. Must be formally adopted at your organizational meeting.
Covers: Board composition, officer roles, meeting rules, voting, amendments
Tip: IRS Form 1023 has a sample conflict-of-interest policy to include
Unlocks: Organizational meeting Some bank accounts 501(c)(3) application
5
Hold Organizational Meeting
After Bylaws
The board formally adopts the bylaws, elects officers, and passes initial resolutions. This completes Virginia's organizational requirements and is also good practice before opening a bank account, since many banks ask for a board resolution authorizing signatories.
Actions: Adopt bylaws, elect officers, authorize bank account signatories, adopt conflict of interest policy
Output: Signed meeting minutes — keep these permanently as part of your corporate records
Unlocks: Bank account (with resolution) 501(c)(3) application
6
Set Up Donation Collection
After Bank Account
Once you have a bank account, you can start accepting donations. Donations will not be tax-deductible until 501(c)(3) is approved — but if you file Form 1023 within 27 months of incorporation, approval is retroactive to March 4, 2026, so donors who gave earlier may still be able to deduct.
Zeffy: 100% free, built for nonprofits, no 501(c)(3) required — recommended
PayPal: Business account, widely trusted, ~2.9% fees until 501(c)(3)
Be transparent with early donors that 501(c)(3) is pending and that deductibility will be retroactive to March 4, 2026 once approved.
Phase 3 — Federal Tax Exemption
7
Apply for Federal 501(c)(3) Tax-Exempt Status
After Org Meeting
This is the IRS application for federal tax-exempt status. Once approved, donations become tax-deductible and you become eligible for nonprofit grants, discounts, and programs like Google Workspace for Nonprofits.
Form: 1023-EZ ($275, streamlined) or Form 1023 ($600, full) — 1023-EZ requires passing the IRS eligibility worksheet (receipts/assets limits and other criteria apply)
Need: EIN, organizing document (Certificate of Incorporation), adopted bylaws, description of programs and activities
Timeline: 3–6 months for IRS review (can be faster with 1023-EZ)
Deadline: File within 27 months of incorporation (by June 2028) for retroactive status back to March 4, 2026
Unlocks: Tax-deductible donations Google Workspace for Nonprofits Grant eligibility Nonprofit discounts
8
Apply for Virginia Retail Sales & Use Tax Exemption
After 501(c)(3)
Virginia 501(c)(3) organizations are generally exempt from Virginia corporate income tax automatically once the federal determination letter is issued — no separate income tax application is needed. However, sales and use tax exemption requires a separate state application.
Where: tax.virginia.gov — Nonprofit Online portal or Form NP-1
Need: IRS determination letter, EIN, Certificate of Incorporation
Phase 4 — After 501(c)(3) Approval
9
Google Workspace for Nonprofits
After 501(c)(3)
Free Google Workspace account including Gmail with custom @setd5syndrome.org addresses, Google Drive, Docs, Calendar, and Meet. Requires the IRS determination letter confirming 501(c)(3) status.
Apply: google.com/nonprofits — eligibility is verified through Goodstack (Google's current verification partner)
10
PayPal Giving Fund & Discounted Rates
After 501(c)(3)
Enroll in PayPal Giving Fund — PayPal does not charge charities or donors fees through the Giving Fund, though some partner platform fees may still apply. PayPal's confirmed charity rate for direct processing is 1.99% + $0.49 per transaction. Stripe offers a nonprofit discount rate after 501(c)(3) verification — contact Stripe directly for current pricing, as they do not publish the rate publicly.
