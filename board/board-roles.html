SETD5 Syndrome Foundation, Inc.

Board Roles & Responsibilities

Reference guide for the Organizational Meeting — Bylaws Adoption

Virginia Nonstock Corporation
Governing document: Bylaws_Final
Articles III, IV & V
Prepared: March 2026
All board members hold equal voting power regardless of title. The roles below describe governance responsibilities, not authority over one another. Every director — whether Chair, Treasurer, or at-large member — carries the same three fiduciary duties and the same legal accountability for the organization's conduct.

Required Officer Positions

Required by Bylaws
Chair
Required

Presides over all board meetings, sets agendas, and signs official documents on behalf of the organization. Serves as the primary point of contact between the board and any staff or executive director.

  • Facilitate all regular and special board meetings
  • Sign contracts, deeds, and formal instruments authorized by the board
  • Serve as liaison between the board and the Executive Director (if hired)
  • May delegate signing authority to another officer with board approval
The Chair holds one vote equal to all other directors. The role confers presiding authority, not supervisory authority over fellow board members.
Secretary
Required

The official keeper of records. Responsible for accurate minutes of every board meeting and maintaining the organization's governing documents.

  • Take and maintain accurate minutes of all board meetings
  • Distribute draft minutes to directors at least 7 days before the next meeting
  • Maintain the corporation's official records (bylaws, resolutions, articles)
  • Send required meeting notices to directors
An active operational role carrying ongoing recordkeeping obligations between meetings, not solely during them.
Treasurer
Required

Owns the financial health of the organization. Oversees all accounts, ensures accurate recordkeeping, and keeps the board informed on financial standing at every meeting.

  • Maintain full and accurate financial records of all receipts and disbursements
  • Present financial reports to the board at every regular meeting
  • Oversee preparation of the annual budget
  • Ensure compliance with IRS Form 990 and applicable tax obligations
  • Chair the Finance Committee if one is established
This position requires sufficient financial acumen to fulfill its oversight obligations. All financial records of the Corporation shall be maintained at the principal place of business and made available for inspection by any director upon reasonable advance notice.

Director (At-Large)

All Board Members
Director / Board Member
Full Voting Member

Every person on the board — including all officers above — is first and foremost a Director with equal voting rights and equal legal responsibility. At-large members hold no officer title but carry identical authority, obligations, and accountability.

  • Attend and vote at all regular and special board meetings
  • Serve on and chair committees as assigned
  • Contribute expertise, connections, and time to the organization's mission
  • Review financial reports and hold the organization accountable
  • Sign and comply with the annual Conflict of Interest Policy
  • Recuse from any vote where a personal financial interest exists
A director serving as committee chair exercises formal delegated authority within that committee's designated scope, constituting a substantive leadership function within the governance structure of the Corporation.

Optional Officer Positions

Board May Elect
Vice Chair
Optional

Steps in for the Chair when unavailable and often leads a specific area of work delegated by the Chair. In many organizations this role develops into the next Chair.

  • Fulfill Chair responsibilities during the Chair's absence or incapacitation
  • Assist the Chair as directed
  • May lead a specific committee or initiative on behalf of the board
President / Executive Director
Optional

The chief executive responsible for day-to-day operations. May be hired from outside the board as the organization grows — this person does not need to be a director.

  • Carry out policies and programs approved by the board
  • Manage day-to-day operations
  • Report to the board; serves as a non-voting participant if not a director
Most small founding boards do not hire an Executive Director initially. This role is typically added later.

Committees

Established by Board Vote
Committees are created by a majority board vote at any time (Article V). Each committee brings recommendations to the full board for collective vote — no committee has the authority to act independently. This means a board member who chairs a committee is not reporting to themselves: they are presenting their committee's work to the board as a governing body, where all members — including the committee chair — deliberate and vote together. A board member may chair or co-chair more than one committee — this is common on small founding boards where expertise and interest naturally overlap across areas.
Nominating & Governance Committee (Required by bylaws)

Recruits board candidates, oversees the annual conflict of interest review, and monitors governance best practices. Consists of a minimum of two directors, appointed annually by the Chair. Brings recommendations to the full board for vote.

Finance Committee

Oversees budgeting, financial controls, audit or Form 990 review, and financial reporting to the board. Chaired by the Treasurer. Brings recommendations to the full board for vote.

Research Committee

Evaluates research priorities, coordinates with the Scientific Advisory Board, reviews grant applications, and monitors funded projects. Brings recommendations to the full board for vote.

Communications & Technology Committee

Oversees the organization's website, digital presence, family-facing content, and communications strategy. Brings recommendations to the full board for vote.

Family Outreach Committee

Connects with affected families, coordinates community support resources, and ensures the family voice informs the board's priorities. Brings recommendations to the full board for vote.

Fundraising & Development Committee

Plans and oversees fundraising campaigns, donor relations, grants strategy, and development activities to support the organization's programs. Brings recommendations to the full board for vote.

Working Groups

Open to Volunteers
Working groups — referred to in the bylaws as ad hoc committees (Article V) — are temporary, task-specific groups formed to accomplish a defined objective and dissolved upon completion. Unlike standing committees, working groups may include non-board members such as volunteers, community members, and subject matter experts. They carry no independent governance authority; all formal decisions arising from their work are brought to the full board for vote.
Committees
Board Members Only
  • Created by majority board vote
  • Standing — ongoing scope and responsibilities
  • Chaired by a board member appointed by the Chair
  • Bring recommendations to the full board for vote
  • Membership limited to directors unless otherwise authorized
Working Groups
Open to All
  • Formed informally or by board vote for a specific task
  • Temporary — dissolved when the task is complete
  • May be led by a board member or a designated volunteer
  • No independent authority — findings go to the board for action
  • Open to volunteers, community members, and advisors

Elections & Vacancies

Director Elections
  • Directors serve two (2) year terms with no limit on consecutive terms
  • Terms are staggered where practicable so that not all seats expire in the same year
  • Directors are elected by majority vote of the current board at any duly held meeting
  • The founding board may set initial terms of one (1) and two (2) years respectively to establish staggering
Officer Elections
  • Officers serve two (2) year terms and are eligible for re-election without limit
  • Officers are elected at the Annual Meeting of the Board, held in the first quarter of each calendar year
  • Interim officer vacancies may be filled by board vote at any duly held meeting, effective until the next Annual Meeting
Resignation
  • Any director or officer may resign at any time by providing written notice to the Chair or Secretary
  • Resignation takes effect at the date specified in the notice, or immediately upon receipt if no date is given
  • No minimum notice period is required under the bylaws
Vacancies
  • Any vacancy arising from resignation, removal, death, or expansion of the board may be filled by majority vote of the remaining directors
  • A quorum of the full board is not required to fill a vacancy — the remaining directors may act even if fewer than a quorum remain
  • A director appointed to fill a vacancy serves only the remainder of the unexpired term, not a new full term
Removal
  • Any director may be removed with or without cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the board
  • Removal may only occur at a meeting called for that purpose, and the written meeting notice must state that removal is on the agenda
  • Any officer may be removed with or without cause by a majority vote of the board at any duly held meeting

Fiduciary Duties — All Directors

These three duties apply equally to every board member regardless of title. They are legal obligations under Virginia law, not suggestions.
Duty of Care

Act in good faith and with the care a reasonably prudent person would exercise. Stay informed, attend meetings, and ask questions.

Duty of Loyalty

Put the organization's interests above personal or outside interests. Disclose and recuse from any conflict of interest.

Duty of Obedience

Ensure the organization stays true to its stated mission and complies with applicable laws, bylaws, and its own policies.
